Learning Experience

The biggest challenge I faced with this project was the data cleaning portion. I thought the csv files Etsy provides its sellers would be straightforward, complete, and easy to translate. That my biggest challenge would be learning to calculate a customer cohort analysis in Tableau. I learned the error of my thoughts quite quickly. The csv files were rife with ambiguity in column names and several columns' values that were calculated into the Order Net column were missing from teh Orders table.

Filters, Boolean tests, and XLOOKUPs (I have never appreciated Microsoft 365 so much) were my tools of choice as I slowly merged all the relevant missing transaction figures from the large Etsy Statements table (nearly 100K rows) into the Orders table.

The whole cleaning and transforming process took several hours over a handful of days and by the end I felt nothing but sympathy for sellers on Etsy. The owner hadn't been aware that the gift wrapping service she offered had a fee applied to it if selected. I texted her while I was trying to figure out why I was missing $4 specifically from the Order Net for 50+ rows.

Me: Do you charge a $4 fee for gift wrapping?
Owner: Yes
Me: K
Owner: Next you're going to tell me Etsy charges a 5% fee on that too
Me: Actually...

She laughed, but I thought, a woman who is in the top 1% of sellers, who has been selling on Etsy for over 10 years, isn't even aware of all the fees she's being charged. It felt like Etsy was purposely keeping sellerâ€™s financial data murky to prevent sellers from seeing the entire financial picture of each order placed.

Lesson Learned: Even when you can download all the data directly from the company and it is technically all there, it may not be in a comprehensive format.
